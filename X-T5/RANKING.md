# X-T5 Recipe Bank, Ranking (aesthetic value × popularity)

Method. Two signals:
- Popularity, concrete page-view rank from [Fuji X Weekly's "26 Most Popular Recipes of 2026 (Q1)"](https://fujixweekly.com/2026/03/31/the-26-most-popular-fujifilm-recipes-of-2026-so-far-first-quarter/) where the recipe (or its family) appears. This is the best public proxy for real-world demand. "n/a" = not on that list (original designs or niche/other-source recipes).
- Aesthetic (my score, /10), reasoned judgment on distinctiveness, versatility, faithfulness to the target look, and visual appeal.

Overall rank blends both, leaning on popularity as the market signal for a sellable bank. Newly-designed originals are unproven in the market, so they're placed on aesthetic merit with a flag.

## Reading the ranking

- Tier S are the anchors any sellable bank is built on, and three of the five are now originals. That is the commercially important shift: the top of the bank is increasingly clean IP rather than attributed captures.
- Tier A is where the product lives. 14 of its 20 entries are originals. This is the depth that makes the bank sellable rather than a link list.
- Tier B/C reference recipes are the research library, benchmarking material to be re-derived as originals, not shipping product (see [validation-methodology](../Knowledge/validation-methodology.md)).
- B&W is the best-covered category, spanning every pole: gritty (Tri-X), clean (Acros), reportage (HP5), fine-art slow (Pan F), pushed (Delta 3200, P3200), cinema (Double-X), dramatic (Acros Red).
- Thinnest area: proven-demand colour originals. Most originals carry `n/a` popularity purely because they are new designs with no public page-view signal.

## Caveats
- Popularity is page-view data (interest), not usage, a known-imperfect proxy that Fuji X Weekly states itself. It also covers only FXW recipes, so every original and non-FXW source is structurally `n/a`.
- Aesthetic scores are my reasoned judgment and should be re-weighted against your taste and, ideally, side-by-side field tests. Per [Fujifilm's own validation practice](../Knowledge/validation-methodology.md), the most reliable judgement comes from someone who did *not* build the recipe.
- Scores cluster in 7.0–9.0 because weak recipes were never added. The spread is narrow by construction, treat tiers as more meaningful than decimals.
- For monetisation, the original rows are the clean IP; reference rows are a research/testing library to be re-derived before shipping.
