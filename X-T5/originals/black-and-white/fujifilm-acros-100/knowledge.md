# Fujifilm Neopan Acros 100 — Grade Analysis & Validation

> Original (research + scan validated). See [`recipe.md`](recipe.md), the [B&W README](../README.md), and the [Knowledge base](../../../../Knowledge/README.md).

## The film
Fujifilm Neopan Acros 100 is a fine-grain ISO 100 B&W film famous for the smoothest tonality, near-invisible grain, superb sharpness, and best-in-class reciprocity (long exposures without failure). Fujifilm's Acros film simulation is named after and built to emulate it, making this the most "native" B&W recipe in the bank.

## The look in one line
Clean, smooth, sharp fine-grain B&W with wide tonal gradation and deep-but-detailed blacks, refined and modern, not gritty.

## Real-scan validation (2 scans, 2 light conditions)
Analysed from Wikimedia Commons (analysis-only):
1. Lakeside beach-deck, soft evening light: smooth mid-tone gradation across the wood decking, bright sky holding subtle cloud detail (no blown highlights), deep shadows with detail, grain essentially invisible.
2. Japanese temple wall + trees, harsh sun: rich near-black foliage/trunks *with* edge detail, bright sunlit brick holding full texture, smooth grain even in the dark gravel (Acros's famous grain-free shadows), crisp sharpness.

Confirmed traits → settings: ultra-fine grain → Grain Weak/Small; smooth wide tonality + held highlights → DR200 + Highlight −1; rich detailed blacks → Shadow +1; sharpness → Sharpness +1; smooth (no grit) → Clarity 0.

## Why it's the fine-grain anchor
Against the other two B&W originals, Acros is the clean pole: minimal grain, smooth gradation, Clarity 0. [HP5](../ilford-hp5-plus-400/knowledge.md) adds even visible grain and balanced contrast; [Tri-X](../kodak-tri-x-400/knowledge.md) adds heavy grain, higher contrast, and grit (Clarity +3).

## Datasheet confirmation (Fujifilm Neopan 100 Acros PIB, fetched & extracted)
- Diffuse RMS granularity = 7: extraordinarily fine ("world's highest standard of grain quality" in class) → confirms Grain Weak/Small and the clean texture.
- "Rich gradation and outstanding sharpness"; resolving power 60 lines/mm (1.6:1) to 200 lines/mm (1000:1) → confirms Sharpness +1 and the smooth wide tonality (moderate tone curve, no crushing).
- Superb reciprocity (minimal compensation to 120s+), a genuine Acros signature worth noting for long exposures.
