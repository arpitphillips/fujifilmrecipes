# Ilford Delta 3200 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · B&W · STILLS

Research-backed **original**, derived from the Ilford Delta 3200 Professional datasheet (archived: [`_reference-sources/datasheets/ilford/delta-3200.pdf`](../../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) + a real street scan (analysis-only). Reproduces Ilford's high-speed push stock: **true ISO ~1000, designed to be shot and developed at EI 3200 — the heaviest, most prominent grain of any B&W in the bank, with a smoother, more "organic" push character than Kodak's T-Max P3200.** Derivation in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

### Why these values (short)
- **Acros base** — the premium B&W profile carries Delta's real tonal gradation better than plain Monochrome despite the heavy grain overlay.
- **Grain Strong, Large** — datasheet: true speed **ISO 1000**, "designed to be exposed at EI 3200 and given extended development" (range EI 400–25000) — the heaviest push-driven grain of any B&W here, heavier than Tri-X.
- **DR400** — holds detail across the wide contrast range that push development produces; also nudges the digital ISO floor up, appropriate for a "shoot it fast" stock.
- **Shadow +2** — deep blacks from extended/push development (confirmed in the real scan: clothing and doorway shadows go rich and dark).
- **Highlight 0, Sharpness 0** — kept moderate/neutral (unlike Tri-X's snappier +1/+1) for Delta's comparatively smoother, more "organic" pushed tonality — a deliberate point of difference from both Tri-X and T-Max P3200.
- **Clarity +1** — a light touch of grit befitting the reportage/push character, less than Tri-X's +3 (Delta's identity is about grain volume, not micro-contrast bite).
- **ISO to 12800** — shooting at high ISO (3200+) is the entire point of this stock; treat the ISO dial as part of the recipe, the same way as [T-Max P3200](../../../reference-recipes/kodak-t-max-p3200/recipe.md).

### Handling
- **Night, indoor, low light, reportage** — this is a "shoot it dark" film; don't be shy about high ISO.
- For a punchier/grittier alternative in the same speed class, use [Kodak T-Max P3200](../../../reference-recipes/kodak-t-max-p3200/recipe.md) (Highlight +1/Shadow +3/Sharpness +2) — the two sit side by side as the "smooth" vs "snappy" push-speed B&W options.
