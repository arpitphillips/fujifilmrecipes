# Ilford Pan F Plus 50 — Grade Analysis & Derivation

> Original (datasheet-validated; scan pass pending). See [`recipe.md`](recipe.md), the [B&W README](../README.md), and the [Knowledge base](../../../../Knowledge/README.md).

## The film
Ilford Pan F Plus, the slowest film in Ilford's current range (true ISO 50). The datasheet (archived: [`_reference-sources/datasheets/ilford/pan-f-plus.pdf`](../../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) states plainly: "an extremely fine grain black and white film. It has outstanding resolution, sharpness and edge contrast. These characteristics make it the natural choice where fine detail and lack of grain are more important [than speed]." This is a precision, controlled-light stock, architecture, product, landscape, fine-art portraiture, not a reportage or low-light film.

## The grade in one line
The sharpest, finest, most contrasty B&W in the bank, a crisp, "etched" precision look at the opposite pole from grainy push stocks like Delta 3200 or T-Max P3200.

## How the settings embody it
Acros supplies rich tonality; DR200 + Highlight 0 + Shadow +1 build a firm, contrasty curve (not a soft latitude-forgiving one; Pan F is a controlled-light film). Sharpness +2 is the highest value in the whole bank, and Clarity +2 is the second deliberate break from the Clarity-0 default: the datasheet's own phrase "edge contrast" is precisely what Clarity's micro-contrast processing models, so faithfulness requires using it. The ISO ceiling is capped at 400, far lower than any other B&W recipe here, because pushing an ISO-50 "lack of grain" film to high digital ISO would contradict its entire identity.

## vs the rest of the B&W family
Pan F Plus and Delta 3200 anchor opposite ends of the bank's B&W range: Pan F is slow/fine/sharp/contrasty (controlled light, precision work); Delta 3200 is fast/grainy/smooth-pushed (available light, reportage). Acros 100 and HP5 Plus 400 sit in between as the "everyday" fine and balanced options; Tri-X 400 is the gritty middle-speed reportage classic. See the comparison table in [Delta 3200's knowledge.md](../ilford-delta-3200/knowledge.md#vs-the-rest-of-the-bw-family).
