# Ilford Pan F Plus 50 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · B&W · STILLS

### Why these values (short)
- Acros base: richest tonality of the monochrome sims; the right canvas for a fine-art-grade stock.
- Grain Weak, Small: datasheet: "**extremely fine grain**... These characteristics make it the natural choice where fine detail and lack of grain are more important [than speed]." The finest-grain B&W setting in the bank alongside Acros 100.
- DR200, Highlight 0, Shadow +1: a firm, contrasty, "snappy" curve rather than a soft one; Pan F is not a latitude-forgiving film, it's a controlled-light precision stock.
- Sharpness +2: the highest sharpness value used anywhere in this bank. Datasheet: "outstanding resolution, sharpness, **and edge contrast**."
- Clarity +2: the second deliberate deviation from the Clarity-0 default (after Tri-X). "Edge contrast" in the datasheet's own words is a micro-contrast/acutance property — exactly what Clarity models. Faithfulness demands it here.
- ISO capped at 400: this is the slowest film in the entire bank (true ISO 50); keeping the digital ceiling low preserves the ultra-fine, ultra-clean character. Pushing it to 3200+ would betray the whole point of the stock.

### Handling
- Architecture, product, fine-art, controlled-light portraiture: needs good light; this is not a low-light film.
- For a softer, less "etched" alternative use [Fujifilm Acros 100](../fujifilm-acros-100/recipe.md) (Clarity 0); for a *daylight contrast filter* option try +Ye or +R on this base for classic architectural sky drama.
