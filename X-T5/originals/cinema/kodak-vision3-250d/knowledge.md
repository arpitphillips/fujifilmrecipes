# Kodak Vision3 250D — Grade Analysis

> Original (research-backed). See [`recipe.md`](recipe.md), the [cinema README](../README.md), and the [Knowledge base](../../../../Knowledge/README.md).

## The stock
Kodak Vision3 250D (5207 cine / 7207 16mm) is the **daylight-balanced** medium-speed ECN-2 motion-picture negative — "all the beauty of daylight." It carries Vision3's Sub-Micron Technology (**2 stops extra highlight latitude**) and Dye Layering (**reduced grain and more detail in shadows**). As a camera negative it is inherently **low-contrast and wide-latitude**, designed to be graded; the beloved "look" is that clean, gentle, filmic daylight rendering.

## The grade in one line
**Clean, natural, low-contrast daylight cinema** — soft highlights, open detailed shadows, gentle filmic colour, fine grain.

## How it's built
Eterna gives the low-contrast rich-shadow cine foundation. DR400 + Highlight −2 reproduce the 2-stop highlight latitude (soft roll-off, nothing blown). Shadow 0 keeps the clean, open, detailed shadows Vision3 is known for. Color +2 + CCE Strong lift Eterna's flat saturation to a natural graded look with dimensional skin. Fine grain + Clarity 0 keep it clean and faithful.

## vs the other cinema recipes
- **[Vision3 500T](../kodak-vision3-500t/knowledge.md):** the tungsten night/interior sibling — cooler, higher ISO, moodier.
- **[2383 Print](../kodak-2383-print/knowledge.md):** adds the theatrical *print* teal-orange grade on top of a negative-like base.
- 250D is the **neutral daylight** member — the cleanest, most natural of the three.
