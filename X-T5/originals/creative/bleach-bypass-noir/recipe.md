# Bleach Bypass Noir — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

A silvery, desaturated, high-contrast urban drama look, the bleach-bypass process feel (skipped bleach = retained silver: low saturation *and* high contrast) pushed toward noir street/architecture. Uses the bank's first Eterna Bleach Bypass base, the community's consensus most-underrated film sim. Creative design; see [creative README](../README.md).

| Setting | Value |

| Film Simulation | ETERNA Bleach Bypass |

### Why
- Eterna Bleach Bypass: natively low-saturation + high-contrast (the bleach-bypass signature); everything else just aims it.
- WB −2R/+1B: cold silver-steel cast.
- Shadow +2, DR400, Highlight 0: inky dramatic blacks while the retained-silver highlights stay controlled.
- Color +1: EBB is already drained; +1 keeps the last traces of colour from dying entirely (faces stay human).
- Clarity +2, Sharpness +1, Strong/Small grain: the hard metallic grit (justified deviation: grit is the look).
- Underexpose: noir lives in the shadows; ISO ceiling high for night streets.

### Best use
Urban night/overcast streets, rain, brutalist architecture, moody portraits, partially-cloudy drama. For colour-forward night neon use [CineStill 800T](../../../reference-recipes/cinestill-800t/recipe.md) instead.
