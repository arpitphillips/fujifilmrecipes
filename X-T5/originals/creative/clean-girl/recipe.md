# Clean Girl — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

The clean girl aesthetic: bright, airy, white-dominant, minimal, crisp neutral whites (linen, walls, ceramics), luminous healthy skin with a soft peach glow, gently lifted shadows, and zero texture. The photographic opposite of the bank's gritty film emulations: no grain, no clarity, no cast, polished freshness. Creative original; see [creative README](../README.md).

### Why these values
- Auto White Priority WB: the recipe's engine, and the reason whites stay *white-white*. This is Fujifilm's AWB mode designed specifically to neutralise warm casts and keep whites clean (see [white-balance.md](../../../../Knowledge/white-balance.md)), cream linen and white walls render crisp-neutral instead of drifting yellow indoors. The tiny +1R/−1B shift keeps skin alive so the neutrality never turns clinical.
- Astia base: soft gradation and the most flattering skin of the colour sims; luminous, never harsh.
- DR400 + Highlight −1.5: you're exposing bright (+2/3 to +1⅓), so the whites need maximum headroom and a soft roll-off: bright and luminous *with* fabric/skin detail held, never clipped.
- Shadow −1: gently lifted, airy shadows. Deliberately *not* −2: that reads faded/matte ([Matte Editorial](../matte-editorial/)'s territory); clean girl keeps dimensional freshness.
- Color +1, CCE Weak, CCFXB Off: fresh restraint: healthy lips/blush warmth without vividness; blues stay light and airy rather than deep.
- Grain Off: the defining "clean" choice. The only recipe in the bank with grain fully off besides Kodachrome 25, texture is the enemy of this look.
- Sharpness −1, Clarity 0: soft polish, no halos, no grit; instant saves.
- ISO capped at 1600: cleanliness demands low noise; this look lives in good light (window light, bright daylight, softboxes).

### Shooting notes
- Light is 80% of this aesthetic: big soft sources, window light, open shade, overcast, diffused daylight. Harsh midday sun fights it.
- Expose bright and trust the recipe: DR400 + Highlight −1.5 are built to hold the whites at +1.
- Styling does the rest: whites/creams/beiges in frame reinforce the palette; the recipe keeps them neutral.
- Golden variant (clean girl at golden hour): WB Daylight, +2R & −2B, warmth returns but stays polished.
- More studio polish (crisper editorial version): base PRO Neg. Hi, Highlight −1, a touch more contrast and snap.
