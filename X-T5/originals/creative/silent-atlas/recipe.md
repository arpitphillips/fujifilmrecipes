# Silent Atlas — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

The stills companion to the [video recipe](recipe-video.md): a cinematic documentary teal-and-warm travel-film grade inspired by [Merlin Krumme's "The Silent Atlas"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=peeq2ajU40E), shot in the Atlas Mountains of Morocco. Warm amber and terracotta in the midtones and skin, cool teal shadows, deep rich blacks, soft misty highlights, muted filmic saturation. Full grade analysis in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

The video version was designed first (the reference is a film); this stills build expresses the same grade using the controls movie mode lacks, grain and the Color Chrome effects.

### How this differs from the video version (and why)
| Setting | Video | Stills | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Color | +3 | **+2** | Stills has Color Chrome Effect Strong to carry the warm-tone richness; video compensated with raw saturation (the bank-wide stills↔video convention, applied in reverse) |
| Color Chrome Effect | n/a | **Strong** | Deepens the amber/terracotta earth tones dimensionally instead of just saturating them |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | n/a | **Weak** | Adds teal depth to shadows and misty skies (Strong would over-deepen on X-Trans V's already-deep Classic Neg blues) |
| Grain | n/a | **Weak, Large** | A subtle, organic cine texture. The reference is digitally shot (Sony FX30) and filmic-graded — restrained large-structure grain matches that "film-adjacent" feel; heavy grain would overstate it |
| Everything else | — | same | WB split, DR200, tone curve, softness all transfer directly |

### The Glimmerglass question (Clarity 0)
The reference's gentle highlight bloom comes from a physical Tiffen Glimmerglass diffusion filter. Per the bank's quality standard, Clarity stays at 0 and the physical filter (Glimmerglass, CineBloom 5–10%, or a similar mist filter) is the recommended route. It looks better and costs no save time. If you have no filter, Clarity −2 is the closest in-camera approximation of the diffusion glow, at the cost of slower shot-to-shot saving.

### Shooting notes
- Best in overcast or misty mountains, golden hour, dusty warm-toned villages and earth-toned landscapes. Those are the Atlas conditions, and similar terrain works just as well: Ladakh, rural Spain, adobe and desert architecture.
- In direct harsh sun, switch to DR400 with Highlight −2 to protect against clipping (the ISO floor rises to 500).
- In warm interior light, ease WB to +2R/−2B to stop excessive orange.
- Sibling looks: [Kodak 2383 Print](../../cinema/kodak-2383-print/recipe.md) (theatrical teal-orange, less warm), [Mumbai (General)](../../mumbai/recipe.md) (warm street, more vivid and gritty), [Mumbai Monsoon](../../mumbai-monsoon/recipe.md) (the cool, wet cousin).

> **Provenance:** original creative design targeting the reference film's aesthetic, not a recreation of its Sony/LUT pipeline. The grade comes from a frame-by-frame study of the reference (see [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md)); the stills build was derived 2026-07-16 from the video-first original.
>
> **Frame-verified 2026-07-16 (second independent pass):** 4 actual frames were re-fetched and analysed (mist landscape at roughly 25%, portrait at 50%, clear sky at 75%, plus the hero shot). The pass confirmed the warm golden skin and earth, the deep held shadows, the soft bloomed highlights, the muted saturation, and the fine grain visible in the mist. It also produced one correction: WB Blue moved from −3 to −2, because the reference's skies and mist are consistently teal-cyan. A −3B shift would yellow the atmosphere toward khaki, while −2B retains enough blue for Classic Negative's native cyan lean to render it teal. If your frames read more saturated than the reference (it's notably muted), drop Color to +1.
