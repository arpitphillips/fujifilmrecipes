# Silent Atlas — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

The stills companion to the [video recipe](recipe-video.md) — a cinematic documentary **teal-and-warm travel-film grade** inspired by [Merlin Krumme's "The Silent Atlas"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=peeq2ajU40E) (Atlas Mountains, Morocco): **warm amber/terracotta midtones and skin, cool teal shadows, deep rich blacks, soft misty highlights, muted filmic saturation.** Full grade analysis in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

The video version was designed first (the reference is a film); this stills build expresses the same grade using the controls movie mode lacks — grain and the Color Chrome effects.

### How this differs from the video version (and why)
| Setting | Video | Stills | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Color | +3 | **+2** | Stills has Color Chrome Effect Strong to carry the warm-tone richness; video compensated with raw saturation (the bank-wide stills↔video convention, applied in reverse) |
| Color Chrome Effect | n/a | **Strong** | Deepens the amber/terracotta earth tones dimensionally instead of just saturating them |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | n/a | **Weak** | Adds teal depth to shadows and misty skies (Strong would over-deepen on X-Trans V's already-deep Classic Neg blues) |
| Grain | n/a | **Weak, Large** | A subtle, organic cine texture. The reference is digitally shot (Sony FX30) and filmic-graded — restrained large-structure grain matches that "film-adjacent" feel; heavy grain would overstate it |
| Everything else | — | same | WB split, DR200, tone curve, softness all transfer directly |

### The Glimmerglass question (Clarity 0)
The reference's gentle highlight bloom comes from a physical **Tiffen Glimmerglass** diffusion filter. Per the bank's quality standard, Clarity stays **0** and the physical filter (Glimmerglass, CineBloom 5–10%, or similar mist filter) is the recommended route — it looks better and costs no save-time. **No-filter variant:** Clarity **−2** is the closest in-camera approximation of the diffusion glow, at the cost of slower shot-to-shot saving.

### Shooting notes
- **Best in:** overcast/misty mountains, golden hour, dusty warm-toned villages and earth-toned landscapes — the Atlas conditions. Also suits similar terrain (Ladakh, rural Spain, adobe/desert architecture).
- **Direct harsh sun:** DR400 + Highlight −2 to protect clipping (ISO floor rises to 500).
- **Warm interior light:** ease WB to +2R/−2B to stop excessive orange.
- Sibling looks: [Kodak 2383 Print](../../cinema/kodak-2383-print/recipe.md) (theatrical teal-orange, less warm), [Mumbai (General)](../../mumbai/recipe.md) (warm street, more vivid/gritty), [Mumbai Monsoon](../../mumbai-monsoon/recipe.md) (the cool/wet cousin).
