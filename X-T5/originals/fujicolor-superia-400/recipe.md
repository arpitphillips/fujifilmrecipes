# Fujicolor Superia X-Tra 400 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

An original recipe grounded in the Fujifilm Superia X-Tra 400 datasheet and multiple film reviews (see [`research.md`](research.md)). It reproduces the classic cool, green-leaning "Fuji look" consumer negative: medium contrast and saturation, fine grain, sharp rendering, with Superia's signature cool blue highlights and slight green-magenta cast. The reasoning behind each setting is in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Film Simulation | Classic Negative |
| Grain Effect | Weak, Small |
| Color Chrome Effect | Weak |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | Weak |
| White Balance | Daylight, −1 Red & +1 Blue |
| Dynamic Range | DR200 |
| Highlight (Tone Curve) | −1 |
| Shadow (Tone Curve) | +1 |
| Color | +2 |
| Sharpness | 0 |
| High ISO NR | −4 |
| Clarity | 0 |
| ISO | Auto, up to ISO 6400 |
| Exposure Compensation | 0 to +1/3 (typically) |

### Why these values (short)
- Classic Negative, because Fujifilm explicitly modeled this film sim on Superia. It's the single most faithful base: cool teal shadows, hue separation, the Fuji consumer character.
- Daylight −1R/+1B is a whisper of cool to reproduce Superia's blue highlights and green-magenta lean without wrecking the "excellent skin" the datasheet promises.
- DR200 with Highlight −1 and Shadow +1 gives Superia's medium contrast with good gray balance and latitude.
- Color +2 and CCE Weak keep saturation medium rather than punchy, with modest warm and green depth.
- CCFXB Weak deepens the cool blues Superia is known for.
- Grain Weak/Small and Sharpness 0, since the datasheet stresses fine grain and excellent sharpness. Fine grain, neutral film-sharp edges, nothing softened.
- Clarity 0 per the quality standard.

### Handling
- Best outdoors in daylight, where Superia's fine grain and cool character shine. Grain rises indoors; bump Grain to Strong, Small for a grittier low-light Superia.
- Wide latitude. Box speed to +1/3 over is clean, and it tolerates more.
- On the Superia family: this targets X-Tra 400, the iconic one. Superia 100 and 200 are finer-grained and a touch warmer, Superia 1600 grainier and moodier. Those variants can be derived later.
