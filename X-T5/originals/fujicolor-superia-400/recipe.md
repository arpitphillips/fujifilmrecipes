# Fujicolor Superia X-Tra 400 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

Research-backed **original** recipe grounded in the Fujifilm Superia X-Tra 400 datasheet + multiple film reviews (see [`research.md`](research.md)). Reproduces the classic **cool, green-leaning "Fuji look"** consumer-negative: medium contrast/saturation, fine grain, sharp, with Superia's signature cool blue highlights and slight green-magenta cast. Grade rationale in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Film Simulation | Classic Negative |
| Grain Effect | Weak, Small |
| Color Chrome Effect | Weak |

### Why these values (short)
- **Classic Negative** — Fujifilm explicitly modeled this film sim on Superia; it's the single most faithful base (cool teal shadows, hue separation, Fuji-consumer character).
- **Daylight −1R/+1B** — a whisper cool to reproduce Superia's cool blue highlights / green-magenta lean, without wrecking its "excellent skin" (datasheet).
- **DR200, Highlight −1, Shadow +1** — Superia's *medium* contrast with good gray balance and latitude.
- **Color +2, CCE Weak** — medium saturation (not punchy), warm/green depth kept modest.
- **CCFXB Weak** — deepens the cool blues Superia is known for.
- **Grain Weak/Small, Sharpness 0** — datasheet stresses *fine grain* and *excellent sharpness*; so fine grain + neutral (film-sharp) edges, not softened.
- **Clarity 0** — per quality standard.

### Handling
- **Best outdoors in daylight** — Superia's fine grain and cool character shine; grain rises indoors (bump Grain to **Strong, Small** for a grittier indoor/low-light Superia).
- Wide latitude — box speed to +1/3 over is clean; it tolerates more.
- The Superia family: this targets **X-Tra 400** (the iconic one). Superia 100/200 are finer-grained and a touch warmer; Superia 1600 is grainier/moodier — variants to derive later.
