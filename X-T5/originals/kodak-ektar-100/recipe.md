# Kodak Ektar 100 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

Research-backed original, derived from the Kodak E-4046 datasheet (archived: [`_reference-sources/datasheets/kodak-still/ektar-100_e4046.pdf`](../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) + 2 real scans (analysis-only). Reproduces "the world's finest grain color negative film": ultra-vivid, high-saturation colour with near-invisible grain and high sharpness, the most vivid negative in the bank, closer to a slide film's punch than any other C-neg here. ### Why these values (short)
- Velvia base (tamed), not Classic Chrome: Ektar's defining trait is *saturation*, the opposite direction from Classic Chrome's muted palette. Velvia is the only Fuji sim vivid enough to match Ektar's "ultra-vivid color," softened here toward a negative-film feel (a modest tone curve instead of Velvia's harder slide-film shoulder).
- Grain Weak/Small: datasheet: Print Grain Index <25 at 4×6 (below the visual threshold of graininess), the finest grain of any colour negative in the bank, on par with Acros.
- WB near-neutral (0R/−1B): unlike warm Gold or cool Superia, real Ektar scans show close-to-neutral grays/asphalt; its vividness comes from *saturation*, not a colour-temperature cast.
- DR200 + Highlight −1: moderate softening; Ektar has real contrast and a punchier character than Portra, not a long soft shoulder.
- Shadow 0: punchier, less lifted than Portra; Ektar doesn't have Portra's generous latitude.
- Color +3, CCE Strong: "ultra-vivid" saturation, especially rich in warm hues (confirmed in scans: reds/oranges hit hard).
- Sharpness +1: datasheet: "optimized sharpness," Kodak's Advanced Cubic emulsion technology.
- Clarity 0: per quality standard; nothing in the evidence calls for grit.
- ISO capped at 3200: an ISO 100 "finest grain" film; keep the digital ceiling low to preserve the invisible-grain character.
- Modest EC: real Ektar has *tighter* exposure latitude than Portra; it wants accurate exposure, not generous overexposure.

### Handling
- Best for landscapes, travel, product, architecture: vivid but true colour, not a portrait-first film (skin can go a touch ruddy at Color +3; drop to +1/+2 for people).
- Don't overexpose heavily, unlike Portra/Gold, Ektar punishes generous overexposure with muddier colour.

Validation tier: Datasheet-validated (E-4046: PGI table, saturation/sharpness claims, archived PDF). 2 real scans checked (a saturated autumn street scene, a night long-exposure), confirmed vivid warm-hue saturation, near-neutral grays, fine grain even in a demanding night exposure, and real contrast capability.
