# Mumbai Monsoon — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

An original recipe designed for Mumbai monsoon conditions: flat overcast light, wet reflective streets, rain haze, rain-deepened greens, and bright colour accents (umbrellas, black-and-yellow taxis, signage) against a grey backdrop, often in low light. It's a cinematic cool-shadow, warm-accent grade that adds depth to flat light while keeping the moody, wet atmosphere. Rationale in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Film Simulation | Classic Negative |
| Grain Effect | Strong, Large |
| Color Chrome Effect | Strong |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | Weak |
| White Balance | 6500K, +2 Red & −2 Blue |
| Dynamic Range | DR400 |
| Highlight (Tone Curve) | −2 |
| Shadow (Tone Curve) | +1 |
| Color | +1 |
| Sharpness | −1 |
| High ISO NR | −4 |
| Clarity | 0 |
| ISO | Auto, up to ISO 12800 |
| Exposure Compensation | −1/3 to +1/3 (judge per scene) |

### Why these values (short version)
- Classic Negative, because its hard tonality injects contrast and depth into flat overcast light, and its cool teal-leaning shadows suit wet streets and rain.
- DR400 with Highlight −2, since grey monsoon skies clip easily. This protects them with a soft roll-off and lifts shadow detail in dark scenes.
- Shadow +1 gives moody, slightly deepened blacks without crushing rain and wet-street detail.
- 6500K with +2R/−2B neutralises the blue-grey overcast and adds a touch of warmth to skin and colour accents, setting up the cinematic teal-shadow, warm-highlight split against Classic Neg's cool shadows.
- Color +1, CCE Strong and CCFXB Weak keep colour restrained and moody overall but let accents pop, and deepen the blues and teals of wet surfaces and rain.
- Grain Strong/Large with Sharpness −1 and Clarity 0 builds the atmospheric, humid, filmic texture from grain and soft edges. Clarity stays at 0 per the quality standard; add a physical mist filter for more haze rather than negative Clarity.
- ISO up to 12800, because monsoon days are dark. The raised ceiling handles low light and the grain masks the noise.

### Tuning for the day
- Heavy rain, very moody: Shadow +2 and Color 0 run deeper, greyer, more melancholic.
- Colour-pop street (taxis, umbrellas): Color +2 for livelier accents.
- Blue hour or night rain with neon: drop to 5500K and let the practicals glow, or consider the [CineStill 800T](../../reference-recipes/cinestill-800t/recipe.md) recipe instead for full neon-night work.
