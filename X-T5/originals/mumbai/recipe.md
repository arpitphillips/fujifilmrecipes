# Mumbai (General) — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V) · STILLS

An **original** place-look for Mumbai's warm, dry-season, everyday character: the chaotic, textured, sun-drenched street — earthy rust/brick/terracotta tones, deep dry-season blue skies, and vivid colour-pop accents (tarps, signage, saris, taxis), with warm skin and a hint of golden haze. This is the **warm/vibrant** counterpart to the cool, wet [Mumbai Monsoon](../mumbai-monsoon/recipe.md). Rationale in [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|

### Why these values (short)
- **Classic Negative** — punchy, characterful, warm-capable, filmic; makes earthy tones rich and colour accents pop while keeping a nostalgic street feel.
- **Daylight +3R/−2B** — warm golden lean for the Indian sun/haze, while the blue sky stays blue.
- **DR400 + Highlight −1.5 + Shadow +1** — tames Mumbai's harsh-sun contrast and holds the city's ever-present **sea-haze glow** (milky dusk horizons), while opening deep alley shadows. *(Highlight refined −1 → −1.5 after the 11-image soul study — see [`knowledge.md`](knowledge.md).)*
- **Color +3, CCE Strong, CCFXB Weak** — vivid saturated colour (accents pop, rust/terracotta rich) with a deep dry-season blue sky.
- **Grain Strong/Small, Sharpness +1, Clarity +1** — the gritty, high-texture street. **Clarity +1 is a deliberate deviation from the 0 default** — the textured chaos genuinely benefits from a little mid-tone grit (drop to 0 for a cleaner look).

### Tuning
- **Golden hour / haze:** WB **+4R/−3B**, Shadow **0** — warmer, glowier.
- **Cleaner / less gritty:** Clarity **0**, Grain **Weak, Small**.
- **Punchier midday:** DR **200**, Color **+4**.
- For **wet/overcast monsoon**, switch to [Mumbai Monsoon](../mumbai-monsoon/recipe.md).
