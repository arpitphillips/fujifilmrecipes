# CineStill 400D v2 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Astia base. Emulates **CineStill 400D**, the daylight-balanced sibling to 800T — repackaged Kodak motion-picture negative (understood to derive from the Vision3 250D emulsion, the same relationship 800T has to Vision3 500T) with the remjet layer removed for C-41 processing. This "v2" is a joint effort between Ritchie Roesch and Nestor Pool, tuned against real film examples; less contrast and a more red/purple lean than v1. Fills the bank's daylight-CineStill gap (800T already covered).

[Source](https://fujixweekly.com/2023/01/17/cinestill-400d-v2-a-fujifilm-x-trans-iv-v-film-simulation-recipe/)

**Notes**
- Especially suited to **golden hour**; also works overcast, shade, and midday sun. Popular for urban/street photography.
- The unusual **Fluorescent 1 WB** (rather than Daylight) is a deliberate creative choice, not a literal light match — it's what pushes the recipe toward 400D's red/purple-leaning cast.
- Compatible with X-Trans IV (X-Pro3, X100V, X-T4, X-S10, X-E4, X-T30 II) and V; **not** compatible with X-T3/X-T30.

> **Datasheet check (2026-07-16):** No CineStill-specific sheet exists (CineStill doesn't publish one), but the underlying emulsion is understood to be **Kodak Vision3 250D** with remjet removed and re-rated — the same relationship 800T has to Vision3 500T. Vision3 250D's archived sheet ([`kodak-motion/vision3-250d-5207.pdf`](../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) specifies daylight 5500K balance and a 2-stop extended highlight latitude; this recipe's Highlight −2 / Clarity −4 softness is consistent with that extended shoulder plus the remjet-removal glow/desaturation effect documented for [CineStill 800T](../cinestill-800t/) vs [Vision3 500T](../../originals/cinema/kodak-vision3-500t/).
