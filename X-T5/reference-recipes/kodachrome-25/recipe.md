# Kodachrome 25 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Classic Chrome base. Emulates Kodak Kodachrome 25 (introduced 1974, replacing Kodachrome II), probably the sharpest colour film ever made, at the cost of a punishingly low ISO. Completes the bank's Kodachrome family alongside [Kodachrome 64](../kodachrome-64/) and [Vintage Kodachrome](../vintage-kodachrome/) (1st-era).

[Source](https://fujixweekly.com/2023/03/06/kodachrome-25-fujifilm-x-t5-x-trans-v-film-simulation-recipe/)

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Film Simulation | Classic Chrome |
| Grain Effect | Off |
| Color Chrome Effect | Strong |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | Weak |
| White Balance | Daylight, +2 Red & −4 Blue |
| Dynamic Range | DR400 |
| Highlight | +0.5 |
| Shadow | −0.5 |
| Color | +1 |
| Sharpness | +3 |
| High ISO NR | −4 |
| Clarity | +3 |
| ISO | Auto, up to ISO 1600 |
| Exposure Comp | 0 to +2/3 (typically) |

Notes
- Sharpness +3 and Grain Off are the headline traits. This is the sharpest recipe in the entire bank, matching K25's reputation as the sharpest colour film ever made.
- ISO capped at 1600 reflects the real film's punishingly slow speed (ISO 25), even generously "pushed" digitally, this recipe stays conservative.
- X-Trans V adaptation of the X-Trans IV original: Color Chrome FX Blue Weak instead of Strong (V renders Classic Chrome blues deeper).

> **Datasheet check (2026-07-16):** Kodak's combined K25/64/200 sheet (E-55, archived: [`kodak-still/kodachrome-25-64-200_e55.pdf`](../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) gives K25 the finest granularity of the three (RMS 9, vs K64's RMS 10 and K200's RMS 16). Grain Off here is even more conservative than the RMS-9 anchor would strictly demand (Weak/Small would be the literal match, per the [validation methodology's grain ladder](../../../Knowledge/validation-methodology.md)), consistent with the recipe author's choice to push sharpness/clarity instead of relying on grain texture.
