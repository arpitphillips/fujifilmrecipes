# Kodak Plus-X 125 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Acros base. Emulates Kodak Plus-X 125 (mid-century, discontinued 2011), a classic mid-speed pictorial B&W, softer and gentler than Tri-X, long a favourite of Kodak's own product/catalogue photography and mid-century photojournalism. Created by Dylan Van Matre, who studied the official spectral-sensitivity chart to derive the white balance and field-tested for two years. Rounds out the bank's classic B&W speed range alongside [Pan F Plus 50](../../originals/black-and-white/ilford-pan-f-plus-50/) (slower) and [Tri-X 400](../../originals/black-and-white/kodak-tri-x-400/) (faster).

[Source](https://fujixweekly.com/2025/09/21/kodak-plus-x-125-fujifilm-x-trans-iv-x-trans-v-film-simulation-recipe/)

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Film Simulation | Acros (or +Ye/+R/+G) |
| Grain Effect | Weak, Large |
| Color Chrome Effect | Strong |
| Color Chrome FX Blue | Off |
| White Balance | Daylight, +9 Red & +9 Blue |
| Dynamic Range | DR200 |
| Highlight | −1 |
| Shadow | +1 |
| Monochromatic Color | WC 0 & MG 0 (Off) |
| Sharpness | −1 |
| High ISO NR | −4 |
| Clarity | +2 |
| ISO | Auto, up to ISO 6400 |
| Exposure Comp | **−1/3 to −2/3** (typically — negative convention) |

Notes
- Unusual +9 Red & +9 Blue WB shift, both pushed to their positive extremes simultaneously, was derived by the author directly from Plus-X's published spectral-sensitivity curve, not by eye.
- Negative exposure-comp convention (like [Ilford FP4 Plus 125](../ilford-fp4-plus-125/)), slight underexposure protects highlights and builds the gentle-but-defined contrast Plus-X is known for.
- Weak, Large grain: an unusual combination (fine roughness, large size) that reads as soft-edged but visible grain clumps, distinct from the crisp small grain of Pan F or Acros.
- Compatible with X-Trans IV (except X-T3/X-T30) and X-Trans V, plus newer GFX.

> **Datasheet check (2026-07-16):** Kodak's Plus-X Pan sheet (F-8, archived: [`kodak-still/plus-x-pan_f8.pdf`](../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) describes a classic medium-speed pictorial curve. The recipe's negative EC convention and moderate Highlight/Shadow (−1/+1) are consistent with a gentle, forgiving mid-century B&W stock.
