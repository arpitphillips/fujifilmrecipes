# Kodak T-Max P3200 — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Acros base. Emulates **Kodak T-Max P3200** (1988; discontinued 2012; revived 2018) — the push-process king: a nominal ISO 800 stock designed to be pushed two stops, beloved by news photographers for bad light. Built by Anders Lindborg with John Sevigny, a former news photographer who shot P3200 daily and supplied 25-year-old scans. Fills the bank's high-ISO / pushed-B&W gap.

[Source](https://fujixweekly.com/2023/05/18/kodak-t-max-p3200-a-fujifilm-film-simulation-recipe-for-x-trans-iv-v/)

**Notes**
- **ISO is the creative control:** ~640 ≈ unpushed film; 3200–12800 ≈ progressively pushed processing (more grain + contrast). This exploits the ACROS grain model's ISO-compounding behaviour (see [Knowledge/color-science](../../../Knowledge/color-science-why-film-cannot-be-faked.md)).
- Shadow +3 is the maximum — deep crushed blacks are the point.
- Sits opposite Tri-X in the B&W bank: Tri-X = gritty *daylight* street; P3200 = *night/indoor* reportage.
