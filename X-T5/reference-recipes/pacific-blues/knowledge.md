# Pacific Blues — Grade Analysis & Understanding

> Read alongside [`recipe.md`](recipe.md) and the [Knowledge base](../../../Knowledge/README.md).

## Origin & intent
Not an emulation of a specific film — a **stylised colour grade** built to echo photographer **Lucy Laucht's "Spirit of Summer" / Positano Blues** series: bright, airy coastal images with cool, deep blues and a slightly faded, dreamy feel. Despite the "summer beach" origin it's prized as a **highly versatile** recipe (fog, overcast, desert, gardens, portraits, even night).

## The grade in one line
A **cool, airy, blue-leaning grade** with deep shadows, soft highlights, saturated-but-not-loud colour, and heavy film grain — dreamy coastal/overcast mood.

## How the settings build that grade
| Setting | Value | What it contributes |
|---|---|---|
| **Classic Negative** base | — | Fujicolor Superia character: hard tonality, hue separation, cool-leaning blues/greens. See [film-simulations](../../../Knowledge/film-simulations.md). |
| **White Balance** | **5800K, +1R / −3B** | A cool-neutral daylight Kelvin, nudged slightly to control the cast — lets Classic Neg's blues lead. |
| **Dynamic Range** | **DR400** | Soft highlight roll-off (airy, bright top end); ISO floor 500. |
| **Highlight** | −2 | Strongly softened highlights → the bright, hazy, faded quality. |
| **Shadow** | +3 | *Deep, rich shadows* → the moody, weighty bottom end. The contrast between soft highlights and deep shadows is the recipe's signature. |
| **Color** | +4 | High saturation → the vivid blues and greens pop. |
| **Color Chrome Effect** | Strong | Keeps the saturated warm/green tones dimensional. |
| **Color Chrome FX Blue** | **Weak** | Deepens blues (the whole point) — reduced from Strong for X-Trans V. |
| **Sharpness** | −2 | Soft outlines. |
| **Clarity** | −3 | Low mid-tone contrast → dreamy, slightly hazy. |
| **Grain** | **Strong, Large** | Heavy, coarse grain → analog texture and mood. |
| **High ISO NR** | −4 | Preserve detail/grain. |

## Reading the tonal/colour signature
- **Contrast:** a *split* curve — very soft highlights (−2) over very deep shadows (+3). This gives images punch and mood at once without harsh mids.
- **Saturation:** high (Color +4) but the Classic Neg base keeps it "crunchy" rather than glossy.
- **Cast:** cool, blue-forward — the identity of the recipe (5800K + Classic Neg's cool blues + CCFXB Weak deepening them).
- **Texture:** the grainiest, most overtly analog of the colour recipes (Strong/Large grain + −3 clarity + −2 sharpness).

## Best use
Extremely flexible: **beach, water, and skies** (where the blues shine), plus **fog, overcast, gardens, autumn, and dramatic portraits**. The deep-shadow/soft-highlight combo suits high-key coastal scenes and moody weather equally.

## X-Trans V note
X-Trans V port of the X-Trans IV Pacific Blues; the IV→V change was **Color Chrome FX Blue Strong → Weak** (X-Trans V deepens Classic Neg blues). See [conversion notes](../../../Knowledge/x-trans-v-and-conversion.md).
