# Provia Positive — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Provia base. Emulates vintage Fujichrome Provia / Sensia colour transparency (slide) film of the late 1990s–early 2000s, Ritchie Roesch's refinement of Dan Allen's "Spring Provia," retuned after studying hundreds of real Magnum colour transparencies at Fujikina Copenhagen. The author calls it "hands-down my favorite Provia Recipe of all-time." Fills the bank's slide-film gap for the *standard* Fuji chrome look.

[Source](https://fujixweekly.com/2026/05/27/fujifilm-recipe-provia-positive/)

Notes
- Highly versatile: landscapes, still-life, street. Portraits are hit-or-miss (slide-film saturation on skin).
- On X-Pro3 / X100V use plain Auto WB (no Auto White Priority); X-T3/X-T30 should use the original Spring Provia recipe instead.

> **Datasheet check (2026-07-15):** Fujifilm's Provia 100F sheet specifies RMS 8 (extremely fine) and "vivid *and faithful*" colour, Grain Strong/Small and Color +4 overstate fresh Provia. Deliberate: the author targets *aged vintage transparencies*, not fresh film (documented above). A fresh-Provia faithful build would use Grain Weak/Small, Color +2.
