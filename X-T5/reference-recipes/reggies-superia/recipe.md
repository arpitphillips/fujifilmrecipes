# Reggie's Superia — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Classic Negative base. Not a film emulation but a **deliberately moody, high-contrast, grain-forward** recipe by Reggie Ballesteros (creator of [Reggie's Portra](../reggies-portra/)) — the sequel to that recipe, but with an **opposite design philosophy**: where Reggie's Portra is tuned for versatility across lighting, Reggie's Superia is intentionally **heavy-handed**, prioritising a vintage, film-like aesthetic over flat, detail-preserving imagery. It's also specifically fine-tuned to render **melanated skin tones** accurately, addressing a gap he found in most Classic Negative recipes (his own and others'), which tend to be calibrated for street/landscape rather than portraiture. Subject of his video ["This Fujifilm film simulation recipe made me stop shooting RAW: Reggie's Superia"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rHeIWWcOUwI).

> **Source discrepancy, noted rather than silently resolved:** the FXW article text gives the Blue shift as **−3**; a timestamped walkthrough of Reggie's own video (where he reads the values off his camera screen, 8:52–8:58) gives **−4**. The table above uses the video's value as primary since it's a direct on-screen reading rather than a secondary write-up, but this is a 1-stop-of-precision discrepancy worth being aware of — if your results skew warmer/cooler than expected, try the other value.

**Notes**
- **Philosophy — the opposite of Reggie's Portra:** Portra prioritises versatility and adapts across lighting; Superia is **intentionally heavy-handed** — moody, high-contrast, grain-forward, chasing a vintage film feel rather than a clean, flat, "documentary-accurate" rendering. Don't expect it to behave gently in mixed light the way Portra does.
- **Purpose-built for portraiture on Classic Negative**, not a Superia film emulation — designed to give depth and richness to melanated skin specifically.
- **Color Chrome FX Blue: Strong** — unusually high; on X-Trans V this is a deliberate choice (most CCFXB-Strong recipes in this bank are IV-era and get dropped one notch for V). Keeping it Strong here pushes rich, deep blue depth rather than following the standard IV→V conversion rule.
- **Auto WB** — adapts per scene, but combined with the heavy grain/contrast/CCE settings the *overall* result still reads as deliberately stylised, not neutral.
- **Clarity kept at 0 — explicitly to avoid the camera's post-processing/buffer delay**, not because the look doesn't want grit. For the vintage glow/shadow-lift that Clarity would otherwise provide, Reggie recommends a **physical diffusion filter** (Moment CineBloom or Urth Ethereal) instead.
- **Shoot Fine + RAW** — his recommended workflow, so frames can be reprocessed later in Fuji X RAW Studio if the SOOC JPEG needs adjustment.
- Works on any Fujifilm camera with Classic Negative; compatible with X-Trans V natively and X-Trans IV (except X-T3/X-T30) with slightly different results.

> **Datasheet check (2026-07-16):** Fujifilm's Superia X-Tra 400 sheet (archived: [`fujifilm/fujicolor-superia-xtra-400.pdf`](../../_reference-sources/datasheets/README.md)) describes fine grain and excellent sharpness — this recipe's **Strong/Large grain** and **Sharpness −2** run considerably softer/grainier than the emulsion spec. That's expected and correct: this is explicitly a *heavy-handed portrait interpretation*, not an emulsion-faithful build (see comparison in [`../../originals/fujicolor-superia-400/knowledge.md`](../../originals/fujicolor-superia-400/knowledge.md)).
