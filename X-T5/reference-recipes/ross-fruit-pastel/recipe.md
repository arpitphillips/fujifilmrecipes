# Fruit Pastel (Ross McConaghy) — Fujifilm X-T5 (X-Trans V)

Velvia base. A "light, sweet, fruit-coloured, sugar-covered" pastel look, the community-loved trick of pairing a *vivid* base (Velvia) with *lifted shadows* and grain so saturated colours read as sugared pastels rather than punchy. Created by Ross McConaghy. This is the Velvia-based counterpart to our Astia-based [Pastel Dream](../../originals/creative/pastel-dream/) original (whose "Fruit Pastel energy" variant note points here).

Notes
- The pastel formula: Velvia's vivid saturation, but Shadow −2 lifts the blacks and DR400 flattens the top end, so the strong colours sit in a bright, low-contrast frame and read as *sweet/pastel* rather than bold. Best in high-contrast scenes with prominent colours.
- Contrast with [Pastel Dream](../../originals/creative/pastel-dream/) (Astia, softer/gentler) and [BewareMyVelvia (Bold)](../../originals/creative/bewaremyvelvia-bold/) (Velvia but *bold*, not pastel), Fruit Pastel sits between them: Velvia's punch, pastel's lift.

> **X-Trans V, verified correct as-published (2026-07-16).** Doubly safe: (1) Ross authors on an X-Trans V-category body (proven by his Nostalgic Neg. recipe [Pumpkin Soup](../ross-pumpkin-soup/)); and (2) Velvia is not a blue-affected sim anyway (only Classic Chrome/Neg/Eterna/Eterna Bleach Bypass change between IV and V; see [x-trans-v-and-conversion.md](../../../Knowledge/x-trans-v-and-conversion.md)), so CCFXB Strong transfers to the X-T5 unchanged regardless of source generation.
