# Turning the Recipe Bank into a Gumroad Product

Go-to-market notes for selling this recipe bank on Gumroad, checked against three live competitors — a free mega-blog, a pay-what-you-want aggregator pack, and a single creator selling on reputation alone — rather than assumed.

*Researched 2026-07-15, against 4 live listings. See [Sources](#sources) at the bottom for the exact pages checked.*

## Bottom line

- **The "just give me the settings" market is already free.** A 70-recipe FP1 pack on Gumroad prices itself at pay-what-you-want starting from ₹0. Competing on recipe count is competing for nothing.
- **The one thing nobody else in this space does:** trace every setting back to a manufacturer datasheet, and scan-validate the flagship few against real film. That's a checkable claim none of the comps below can make.
- **Reggie Ballesteros sells single recipes for ~$11** almost entirely on the strength of an audience that already trusts him. This project doesn't have that audience yet — the product itself has to do the trust-building his name currently does for free.
- **Gumroad sends zero organic traffic.** Every sale arrives from somewhere specifically sent from — that's a bigger lever than pricing or packaging.

## 1. Who else is selling this, and what people are actually paying for

| Who | Price | What's actually sold | Where the money really comes from |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fuji X Weekly** (fujixweekly.com) | Free blog; $19.99/yr Patron | The Patron tier unlocks app filtering/favoriting and ~14 early-access recipes — every recipe eventually goes free to everyone regardless. | Volume of free content converting a small % of a huge readership into $20/yr superfans. |
| **"70 Custom Recipes" FP1 pack** (Gumroad aggregator) | ₹0.00+ (name-your-price) | Nothing original — 70 recipes credited to 12 different creators, pre-converted into importable `.fp1` files. Cover image was a stock photo unrelated to the product. | Time saved not retyping settings. No curation, no validation, no proof — a convenience wrapper around other people's free work. |
| **Reggie's Superia** (reggiebphoto.gumroad.com) | ₹963 (~$11) | One recipe, a 1-page usage guide, a handful of example photos — sold as "early access" before the same recipe goes free on YouTube. | Almost entirely parasocial: 13/13 five-star reviews, and nearly every one says a version of "thank you for your work," not "this was hard to find elsewhere." |
| **This bank** (proposed) | See §5 | Recipes traced to manufacturer datasheets, the top tier scan-validated against real film, plus a knowledge layer explaining *why* each setting exists, and honest originals-vs-reference labeling throughout. | Has to be the trust the product itself earns — there's no existing audience to lean on yet, unlike Reggie's. |

The pattern: recipe *settings* are already commodity-free. What people actually pay for is either convenience (FP1 files) or trust in a specific human (Reggie). Neither is "more recipes."

## 2. What's actually being sold

Not volume — that's already free. Four things this bank can claim that none of the comps above can:

**The proof.** Every original ties to a manufacturer characteristic curve, an RMS granularity or Print Grain Index number, and (for the top tier) real film scans. State that on the label — it's checkable, not marketing copy.

**The "why."** The knowledge layer — why film can't be faked in Photoshop, the actual chemistry behind grain, how WB shift/DR/tone curve mechanically combine — turns a settings table into something a buyer learns from. Closer to a masterclass than a cheat sheet.

**The honesty.** Explicit *original, validated* vs. *reference, attributed* labeling, in a market where the aggregator pack blends 70 other people's work into one listing with attribution as an opt-out footnote.

**The curation.** A ranked "start here" set instead of a 70-item wall of undifferentiated choice — the aggregator's real weakness is that nothing tells a buyer which of the 70 to actually use first.

## 3. What to package first

Resist shipping everything in the bank as one giant pack — that's the aggregator's move, and it's already been priced to zero. Ship a coherent, provably-researched core instead.

**The flagship (paid).** The already-validated originals — Kodak Gold 200 (scan-validated), Fujicolor Superia 400, Kodak Portra 400 and Ektachrome E100 (datasheet-derived), the cinema trio (Vision3 250D/500T + 2383 Print), and the B&W trio (Acros 100 / HP5 Plus / Tri-X 400). A coherent, provably-researched core — not the full ~60-recipe bank.

**The lead magnet (free).** Give away one recipe — Gold 200, the most rigorously proven — as a clean, no-charge PDF. Its job is an email address and a first impression of the packaging quality, not a sale.

**The long tail (later).** Split the reference-recipes library into small themed packs once a few are re-derived toward originals — a B&W pack, a cinema pack, a portrait pack. Each is a new discovery entry point, and it compounds: sellers with 11+ listed products average roughly **19× the sales** of single-product sellers on Gumroad specifically.

## 4. Packaging: what an average buyer needs beyond a settings table

Most buyers are not power users who read Fuji X Weekly weekly. The gap between "a table of numbers" and "something an average person can actually use" is where most of the packaging value lives:

| What to include | Why it matters more than the recipe itself |
|---|---|
| Camera-menu breadcrumbs | Not just "Highlight −2" — where in Image Quality → Tone Curve that lives. A first-time recipe user doesn't know the menu tree. |
| A 3-recipe "start here" | Removes the choice paralysis a 60-item list creates. Most buyers want to be told, not to choose. |
| Before/after real photos | The single biggest upgrade over a bare settings table — proof the recipe does what it claims, ideally next to an actual film scan. |
| A one-line "why" per setting | This bank already writes this for every recipe. Neither comp above does. |
| Troubleshooting | "My sky looks too blue" → the one dial to turn. Converts a one-time buyer into someone who doesn't feel stuck. |
| Validation badges | Datasheet / Characteristic / Scan — a visible, specific trust signal instead of a vague "professionally crafted" claim. |
| `.fp1` files | Table stakes now that a free competitor offers direct import — include it, but don't lead with it; it's not the differentiator. |

## 5. Pricing, anchored to what's actually being paid

Real anchors: the aggregator floors at $0 for bare settings. Reggie gets ~$11 for one recipe purely on reputation.

| Tier | Price | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| Lead magnet | Free | One recipe (Gold 200), full PDF quality, no paywall. Email capture, not revenue. |
| **Core pack** | **$12–18** | The validated originals — PDF + FP1 + before/after + decision guide. Priced at Reggie's single-recipe rate, for ten-plus datasheet-and-scan-backed recipes. |
| Extended pack | $22–29 | Adds the reference-recipe archive once more are re-derived. Framed as "the research library," not the headline offer. |

**Skip pay-what-you-want.** It works for the aggregator and for Reggie because people are tipping either goodwill or an existing relationship. A stranger's product with a $0 floor reads as "maybe not worth much" rather than "generous."

## 6. Traffic — the part Gumroad won't do

The single fact to plan around: **Gumroad has no real marketplace browsing** — nobody finds a listing by wandering the site. Every buyer arrives from somewhere specific.

| Channel | Why it fits this product | What to actually post |
|---|---|---|
| SEO article | The knowledge-layer content is genuinely researched and can rank — a bare settings dump can't. | 1–2 long pieces (e.g. "why Fujifilm sims can't be faked in Photoshop") that teach something real, pack linked at the bottom, not gated. |
| Pinterest | Photographers browse Pinterest in a buying mindset, and outbound linking is actively rewarded there. | One pin per recipe, before/after grid format. |
| r/Fujifilm, recipe Discords/FB groups | The exact buyer already hangs out here. | Show the validation methodology or a real scan comparison; disclose authorship, don't just drop a link. |
| YouTube | Recipes are inherently visual; a short demo builds the trust Reggie currently gets for free from an existing channel. | One video per flagship recipe, or a single "how these were validated against datasheets" piece. |
| Gumroad Discover + affiliates | Passive but real, and free. | Tight, keyworded title/tags; offer a revenue cut to a Fuji-focused creator to feature it. |
| Catalog breadth | Not a channel — a multiplier (see §3). Each themed pack is its own entry point. | Split, don't bundle everything into one listing. |

## 7. First moves

1. Get real scans into `references/` for Portra 400, Ektachrome E100, and Superia 400 — Gold 200 is already scan-validated; bring the others up to the same tier before launch.
2. Design the PDF with real typographic hierarchy, actual before/after photos, and visible validation badges — not a bare settings table.
3. Export `.fp1` files for the flagship pack.
4. Publish the free Gold 200 lead magnet plus one SEO article; capture emails.
5. List the core pack on Gumroad with the originals-vs-reference framing stated plainly on the page.
6. Post to two or three communities and Pinterest — only after the above, not before.
