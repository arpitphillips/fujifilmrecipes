About

A film-simulation recipe bank for the Fujifilm X-T5 that treats film looks as something you research, not something you eyeball.

The idea

A recipe is a bundle of in-camera JPEG settings that makes the X-T5 render like a specific film stock — warm Kodak Gold afternoons, cool Superia greens, gritty Tri-X street frames — straight out of camera, no editing. Most recipe collections online are settings someone liked, with a film name attached. This bank works the other way around: start from what the film actually was, then build the look.

Built from the receipts

Every original recipe traces back to something checkable:

  • 📄 The manufacturer's technical datasheet — characteristic curves, RMS granularity, colour balance, exposure latitude. If Kodak measured it in a lab, we used it.
  • 🔬 Real film scans — for the top tier, actual frames shot on the actual stock, compared against the recipe's output and used to tune it.
  • 🧠 A written rationale for every setting — each recipe explains why its grain is Strong/Small or its white balance leans +3 Red, so you can bend it to your taste instead of obeying it.

The validation methodology spells out the evidence tiers behind the badges you'll see on every card — from datasheet-derived up to scan-validated.

Two shelves, clearly labelled

Originals are independently derived here — colour, cinema, black & white, and a set of designed creative looks that don't pretend to be film at all.

Community recipes come from the wider Fujifilm world — Fuji X Weekly, Ross McConaghy, Luís Costa and others. They're here for testing and comparison, every one credited and linked back to its creator.

Why in-camera matters

Fujifilm's film simulations are physics-grounded colour models built by the company that made the actual film — behaviour that can't be honestly replicated with curves and sliders afterwards. (The knowledge base has a whole deep dive on why.) So the JPEG the camera writes is the photograph. The recipe is the darkroom, and it runs in the half-second after you press the shutter.

The fine print

Everything here targets the X-T5 and its X-Trans V sensor generation. Running a recipe on an X-Trans IV body? Raise Color Chrome FX Blue one notch — the conversion guide explains the blue-rendering shift behind that rule.
